Privacy Policy

FitnessTracker iOS · Last updated 2026-04-20

We don't collect your data. The app stores everything on your iPhone and your personal iCloud account. There is no server, no account, no analytics, no tracking.

What we store on your device

  • The workouts you log — sets, reps, weights, RPE, session notes, timestamps.
  • Your preferences — session duration, goals, equipment, training phase, age and body weight if you provide them.
  • The exercise library seeded with the app, plus any per-exercise overrides you set.

Where this data lives

  • On your iPhone, in the app's private sandbox. No other app can read it.
  • Optionally replicated to your personal iCloud via Apple's key-value store — only your Apple ID can read or write it. We have no access to iCloud content.

What we don't do

  • We don't run a server. There is no "our backend."
  • We don't collect analytics. No Firebase, no Mixpanel, no custom telemetry.
  • We don't embed advertising SDKs.
  • We don't request your email, phone number, or account.
  • We don't share your data with third parties.

Apple Health / HealthKit

If you enable the optional "Sync with Apple Health" setting, the app writes completed workouts (type, duration, total volume, estimated calories) into your Health database. The app also reads workouts it previously wrote — scoped to its own source only — so it can update them when you change your body weight or other inputs. It never reads workouts other apps created.

All of this data stays on your device under your control via Apple's Health permissions. You can revoke access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → FitnessTracker.

Data export and deletion

Export. Settings → Export Data gives you every byte the app holds as a JSON file.

Delete. Settings → Reset All Data deletes everything from your device and from your iCloud key-value store. There is nothing for us to delete on our side because we never had a copy.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Crash reports

If you choose to send anonymous crash reports to Apple via iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements, those reports go to Apple, not to us. We receive only the anonymous aggregated reports Apple surfaces in developer tools.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever starts collecting anything new, we will update this page and note the change in a release note. We will not retroactively apply any new collection policy to data already on your device.

Contact

Questions about this policy: open an issue at the public repository where this document lives, or email the developer.
